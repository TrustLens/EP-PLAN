Policyc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/ep-plan#Policy

A generic representation of a collection of rules that may be applicable in the context of a specific plan. A policy can provide a rationale for constraints being applied to steps as well as explain reasons including other plan elements. For example, a data protection policy might require that steps are executed by specific agents and that a data anonymisation step is included in the plan.
has super-classes
Plan Element c
is in domain of
isPolicyOfPlan op
is in range of
includesPolicy op

Rationalec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/ep-plan#Rationale

A generic concept describing a specific justification of a plan or an individual plan element. For example, a plan describing a scientific experiment might be linked to a description of a problem that the experiment is addressing and is the reason why the plan was designed. A data anonymisation step might be included in the plan to protect personal information as a result of a data protection policy associated with a plan (i.e. the reason for including such a step in a plan). Similarly, a data protection policy associated with a plan can be linked to a description of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) which provides the reason why such policy was implemented.
is in domain of
isRationaleOf op
is in range of
hasRationale op

hasConstraintop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/ep-plan#hasConstraint

ep-plan:hasConstraint links ep-plan:Steo to a p-plan:Constraint. It indicates that ep-plan:Activities that will correspond to this step should be evaluated against this constraint. Please note, that we do not stipulate that this constriant must be strictly satisfied before the plan can continue. This is to enable inclusions of constraints defined by policies which can be violated under certain conditions and to also enable capture of incorrect behaviour of a system (e.g. a plan ignored a constriant).
